A karakia is a traditional Māori prayer or chant that is used to acknowledge and give thanks to the gods or ancestors for a particular occasion, event, or activity. A karakia for food blessing, also known as a whakamoemiti, is a prayer that is recited before a meal to show gratitude for the food and to ask for blessings and protection for those who will be consuming it.

use of a karakia for food blessing is common in Māori culture and is often used in gatherings and events where food is being shared. In a corporate setting, a company may use a karakia for food blessing before a company lunch or dinner, or before an important meeting that involves food.

The use of a karakia for food blessing in a corporate setting serves as a way to acknowledge and respect Māori cultural practices, as well as to create a sense of unity and gratitude among the participants. It also shows a commitment to inclusivity and diversity in the workplace, and can help to build positive relationships between different cultures and communities.

We buy fresh and local

Aroha and Mahuta have always been passionate about food, and they have turned their passion into a successful business with their restaurant, The Haukai, located in the beautiful Bay of Islands. The couple had made a conscious decision to buy their ingredients locally, sourcing everything from the farmers and fishermen in the area.

Every day, Aroha would head out to the local markets to handpick the freshest produce, while Mahuta would go to the fishing docks to select the best catch of the day. They took pride in knowing exactly where their ingredients came from and how they were produced.

Their customers appreciated the care and attention that went into every dish at The Haukai. They could taste the difference in the fresh, locally sourced ingredients. The restaurant has become a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, who came for the delicious food and stunning views of the bay.

Aroha and Mahuta loved the close-knit community of the Bay of Islands. They made it a point to collaborate with other local businesses, such as wineries and breweries, to showcase the best of what the area had to offer. The Haukai has become a hub for community gatherings and events, bringing people together over good food and great company.

Despite the challenges of running a restaurant, Aroha and Mahuta were always excited to experiment with new dishes and flavors. They often incorporated traditional Maori ingredients into their dishes, showcasing the unique cultural heritage of the region.

For Aroha and Mahuta, The Haukai wasn't just a business, it was a labor of love. They are proud to be a part of the Bay of Islands community and were committed to showcasing the beauty and bounty of the area through their food.
